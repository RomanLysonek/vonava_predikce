Source: chat discussion comparing ml/solution_final.py vs another_approach/,

Verified facts (don't re-derive, just cite):
- test_data.parquet has NO ProductAvailable column. Forecast is necessarily
  conditional on availability.
- train: 1060/51534 (2.06%) rows ProductAvailable=False. 268 of those have
  Quantity>0 (possible data noise / same-day partial availability).
- Per-product unavailable %: ProductId 24 ~22.0%, 26 ~10.3%, 23 ~9.9%,
  27 ~5.9%, 25 ~5.4%, 22 ~5.3%, 15 ~4.0%. Rest low/zero.
- Zero unavailable rows in the final 56 training days -> the recursive
  history *seed* for the real test forecast isn't directly contaminated;
  contamination mainly affects earlier training/CV windows.
- Contaminated vs availability-clean rolling-28 mean: 3.22% of available
  rows differ, mean abs diff 2.41 (Product 24 alone: 10.69).
- Calendar gaps (missing DATES, not ProductAvailable=False rows) exist for
  ProductId 1 (21 missing days) and ProductId 30 (8 missing days). Both
  gaps sit in the middle of otherwise-continuous, available history (data
  glitch, not a listing gap) -> reindex + treat as NaN/unknown, not zero.
- All 3 ML models (NeuralNet, XGBoost, LightGBM) share recursive_forecast_
  generic (ml/tree_worker.py:89, ml/solution_final.py:165-173) -> the NN's
  bad Christmas fold is NOT proven to be a recursion-specific effect (trees
  recurse too and did better) -- it's an open question (loss fn? capacity?
  single-seed CV vs 3-seed final? under a promo spike).
- CV currently trains/evals a SINGLE NN seed per fold (run_walk_forward_cv
  passes seed=cfg.seed only), but the final submission averages 3 seeds
  (cfg.seeds = 42,123,777). Reported CV "NeuralNet" row does not represent
  the actual submitted estimator. Must fix (Tier A item a3).
- Dynamic Ridge's cap (another_approach/notino_forecasting_solution.py:445)
  applies ONLY to Dynamic Ridge, NOT LightGBM (floor-only, :503) or any
  ensemble. Don't generalize this claim.

Plan tiers (see chat for full rationale/citations):
  Tier A = foundational correctness fixes, no architecture change
  Tier B = structural modeling wins (direct stacked panel, baseline-residual NN, Dynamic Ridge)
  Tier C = ablations, ensemble optimization, dashboard depth, stretch features
